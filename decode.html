Colors and dyes derived from nature embody the simple ecological view of harmony between humans and nature. The six color systems correspond to all things on the plateau, reflecting the survival wisdom of the ancient Xizang people who revered and adapted to nature.

Patterns integrating Tibetan Buddhism and the indigenous Bon religion serve as wearable badges of faith. The Eight Auspicious Symbols, Yongzhong pattern, Tenfold Powerful Symbols and others carry the Xizang people’s pursuit of the spiritual world, expressing wishes for disaster elimination, blessing and lasting good fortune. Woven or embroidered on Bangdian, religious beliefs are integrated into daily attire, becoming an external expression of the Xizang people’s spiritual world.
Bangdian is deeply tied to the five life stages of Xizang women, acting as a ceremonial token of identity transformation. From simple patterns and light colors for young girls to plain patterns and dark colors for the elderly, each stage has its own distinctive Bangdian features. Its style, color and pattern change with life stages, witnessing the complete life journey of Xizang women from growth, marriage and family care to inheritance.
The crafts of hand-spinning, natural dyeing and waist-loom weaving have been passed down orally for thousands of years, serving as a bond for ethnic cultural identity and inheritance. During ethnic events such as the Xizang New Year and Guozhuang dance, women wearing Bangdian are the most distinctive cultural symbol. Today, Bangdian has entered museums and cultural stages, becoming an important carrier for spreading Xizang culture and preserving the ethnic group’s historical memory.

The Life Meaning of Bangdian Patterns

It is a concrete expression of the Tibetan people’s understanding of the essence of life, living conditions and spiritual beliefs, carrying the philosophy and wishes throughout the entire life.

Level 1

Awe & Recognition of Life Origin

The inspiration for Bangdian patterns is rooted in the natural life and primitive beliefs of the plateau, expressing the Tibetan people’s awe for the origin of life:

  • Yongzhong Pattern: Shaped like a rotating sun and flame, symbolizing the eternal reincarnation of life and endless vitality
  • Cross Pattern: Derived from sun worship, symbolizing that sunlight nourishes all things and life grows vigorously
  • Spiral Pattern: Imitating the trajectory of water flow and clouds, symbolizing that life is endless like all things in nature

Conveying the life cognition of "coexistence and prosperity between humans and nature"

Level 2

Guardianship & Blessings for Life Journey

Different patterns guard different stages of life, becoming 'spiritual amulets' for Tibetan women throughout their lives:

  • Maidenhood: A simplified auspicious knot, praying for smooth and harmonious growth
  • Adulthood/Marriage: The Eight Auspicious Symbols pattern, embodying wishes for a happy marriage and a prosperous family
  • Family-Running Stage: The Tenfold Powerful Symbols (Kalachakra) pattern, symbolizing family well-being and harmony between life and the universe
  • Elderly Stage: The simple and primitive Precious Vase pattern, representing a fulfilled life and blessing for future generations

Every pattern carries beautiful wishes for life

Level 3

Inheritance & Continuity of Life

Bangdian patterns are the life link connecting the past and the future, carrying the intergenerational transmission of culture and spirit:

  • When weaving patterns, weave the stories and beliefs passed down from ancestors into the warp and weft
  • Explain the meanings of patterns to pass on the Xizang people’s outlook on life and values
  • Patterns are passed down from generation to generation, continuing the wisdom of life and the root of culture

Let every generation of Xizang people find their spiritual belonging in the patterns

The Exclusive Cultural Meanings of 12 Patterns

The patterns on Bangdian are visual symbols integrating Tibetan Buddhism and indigenous Bon religion, carrying wishes for disaster elimination, blessing and lasting good fortune

1

Auspicious Knot

Two connected swastika emblems, symbolizing unity, harmony and peace, and embodying wishes for a smooth journey for Xizang women’s growth.

2

Wonderful Lotus

A fully bloomed lotus pattern with flowers and fruits, symbolizing nobility and purity, and embodying good wishes for marriage and character.

3

Precious Umbrella

It is meant to eliminate the five poisons of greed, anger, ignorance, arrogance and doubt among all beings, symbolizing saving suffering and guarding the peace of the life journey.

4

Right-Turning Conch

Usually white, symbolizing that the sound of Buddhism spreads everywhere, conveying the spiritual core of coexistence between life and faith.

5

Golden Wheel

Symbolizing the boundlessness and unceasing operation of Buddhism, meaning life is endless and progresses in a cycle like the Dharma wheel.

6

Golden Banner

Symbolizing the supremacy and unshakable nature of Buddhism, enabling ultimate liberation, and embodying wishes for the ultimate perfection of life.

7

Precious Vase

Gathers endless nectar, embraces good deeds and wisdom, can fulfill all beings’ wishes for complete enlightenment, and is a symbol of long-lasting blessings in life.

8

Golden Fish

Symbolizes the state of liberation, and also means recovery, eternal life and rebirth, representing life breaking free from constraints and gaining freedom.

9

Yongzhong Pattern

Also known as the swastika pattern, it is regarded as a symbol of fire and the sun, representing eternal life force and reincarnation, and expressing awe for the origin of life.

10

Cross Pattern

Represents the sun in Xizang culture, with meanings of prosperity, completeness and vigorous growth of life, expressing gratitude for nature nourishing life.

11

Spiral Pattern

Also known as Dog Nose Pattern or Tang Grass Pattern, it looks like a dog’s nose, with rhythmic rotating flowers and leaves, symbolizing that life is endless like all things in nature.

12

Tenfold Powerful Symbols

Composed of seven Sanskrit characters of the Kalachakra Mantra and three additional symbols, it symbolizes the unity of the universe and life, family well-being, and guards the life harmony in the family-running stage.
